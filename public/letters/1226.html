i'm thankful that it is a monday morning and i am still in bed and don't have to go to work. i'm thankful that i slept well last night and though i forgot to turn off our standard alarm clock for 6:42am on weekdays, d and i were able to go back to sleep afterward. i'm thankful that we had nice christmas facetimes with my family and with d's family. i'm thankful that everyone enjoyed the gifts they gave and received. i'm thankful that as an adult, i really do love giving gifts instead of receiving them, that my primary excitement for facetiming with my family was to see them open the things i had gotten them.

i'm thankful that my brother liked the KNEX build your own nerf gun set i got him as a nostalgia present, that his eyes got big in exactly the way i hoped they would. i'm thankful for the soliloquy this prompted from my mom to deborah about how from the age of about 1 1/2, everything my brother and i could get our hands on became a gun: "if they picked up a stick in the yard, the stick was a gun; if they didn't have any objects, their fingers were guns." i'm thankful for one of the first gifts i can really remember wanting, which was a BB gun with a sniper scope. i'm thankful to remember the laser tag sets that my grandparents got us for christmas one year, which we tore through the neighborhood zipping and zapping. i'm thankful that despite all this, my brother and i's very nineties boyish childhood obsession with guns, both of us grew up into vehement nonviolent liberal aesthetes who do not own actual guns.
