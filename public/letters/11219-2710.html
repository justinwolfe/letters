i'm thankful that though i didn't get to do much work on the thing i was planning on and excited to work on yesterday afternoon because other stuff came up, i got to do a little bit, and i'm thankful that though that little bit was derailed by a maddening inconsistent bug that my end-of-the-day brain could not figure out for a while, i eventually figured it out (while refactoring some code to create a stabler base for the thing i was planning to do, i changed a variable name in two of three places, but not the third) and that means that when i go back to working on it, the slate is cleared of that bullshit and i can do the fun and exciting part.

i'm thankful that for dinner last night d made alison roman's quick ragu (

https://cooking.nytimes.com/recipes/1020022-quick-lamb-ragu) (modifications: used pork instead of lamb because we were feeling cheap and used the whole tin of anchovies + the olive oil they came in instead of three) and it was one of the best pasta sauces i have ever had in my life, so powerfully umami and redolent of meat that it made me feel like a ravenous animal out for blood, which, as someone whose appetite has waxed and waned more than usual lately, i was thankful for.

i'm thankful for the first episode of the japan mini-season of queer eye, which we watched during dinner last night and which, as someone who doesn't always find that show quite interesting enough to focus on, was really warm and lovely and inspiring and good top to bottom. i'm thankful for the girlish joy that, when nourished, emerged in full force from inside of the devoted hospice worker who was the subject.
